# SPLENDOR ANALYTICS COMPETITION
## TRIAL ACTIVATION ANALYSIS

Splendor Analytics is a B2B SaaS company that sells a workforce management platform to organization on a subscription basis. 

---

## The Business Model

The business operates on a recurring-basis revenue model where organization pays a recurring fee for continued use of the platform after the free trial period.

But unlike other free trials, for Splendor Analytics, the free trial period is crucial as this is the window of opportunity the company has to convince organizations that there is real value in their product enough for them to pay

So understanding behaviors and patterns during the trial person is sensitive and crucial for the success of this business

---

## What does success looks like for them

Success for them is having a growing base of customer who not only convert after trial periods but stays long enough because a 3 customers who convert and churn 3 months later is far less valuable than one customer who converts and stays for years

If this is the case, it mean just finding patterns and signals that leads to conversion we also need to ensure those signals are causative to ensure they stay even after converting

The  platform covers multiple operational domains so the more functionality an organization used, can be a signal to how well they would convert as the deeper the organization use more of the product functionality the more embedded the product is to their workflow and making it hard to remove. A concept called **Product Stickiness** and very relevant to conversion

---

## The Product

The product is a workforce management platform that enables organizations to plan, track, and manage their day-to-day operations, helping them coordinate teams efficiently, maintain accurate records of work, and ensure smooth execution of payroll and internal processes.

The activity in the dataset are linked to operational outcome but not all activity have the same weight. The activities can be grouped into 3 buckets:

1. **Entry point activity**: These are scheduling activities such as creating shifts, setting availability etc every trialist would most likely use upon onboarding therefore may have little weight but are very sensitive. They are also the foundation of the platform value.

2. **Operational Commitment Activity**: Activities such as time tracking activities like `clocking-in`, `clocking-out...` this layer means the organization has gone beyond exploration to adoption. Absence management is also a key here as it indicates signal genuine integration into operational workflow 

3. **Value realization activity**: Activities like payroll related activates seats at the top of the value hierarchy, as organization would only use a platform for this if they trust the platform. Org that reach here are experiencing the value of the product. Team communication is also a supporting signal in this chain

---

## The Problem

The product team sees that 1 in 5 convert but cannot tell who is on tract to convert, when to intervene or which features actually matters to the decision.

But beyond this the real problem is that Splendor is operating its entire growth engine without feedback. The real problem is a strategy Gap.

The concept of Trial Activation is a solution to that gap. So Trial activation are specific, measurable and evidence-based set of behaviours that constitute activation

---

## User Journey

The ideal journey begins on the first day upon onboarding, with entry activities like creating schedule.

By the end of the second week, the Organization should have least run a full operational cycle, by day 21, the Organization has enough context to make an informed decision then by day 30, the decision is made

But there are many nuance depending on the buying level of the organization. We can group them into 3 bucket:

1. **The committed Evaluator**: They need a tool like this and are only checking if splendor analytics is the right go-to for them

2. **The Curios Explorer**: These are those that Signed up mostly because of the platform looked interesting or a recommendation from someone 

3. **The Slow Starter**: The organization that has genuine need for this product but has slow internal adoption

---

## Key Metrics

The key metrics can be grouped into leading and lagging indicator

Also depending on a context a KPI can change from leading to a lagging indicator
 
### Metrics to watch out for

- **Trial Conversion Rate**: Percentage of Organization that convert after Trial period  
- **Trial Activation Rate**: What percentage of Trialist meets the activation criteria  
- **Time to Activation**: How quickly and Organization reaches activation  
- **Time to Conversion**  
- **Feature Adoption**: What percentage of Trialist use each distinct areas of the platform at least once  
- **Engagement Depth**: How many distinct activity type does an organization used within the trial period  

---

## What does a Successful Trials looks for them

A high-performing trial system is one where the company can reliably identify the behaviors that lead to this value realization, and can intervene at the right moments to guide users toward it.

In regards to this the product team at splendor need 3 decision making layer:

1. In the short term, which customer are at the risk of not converting so the team can intervene with something targeted and useful  

2. In the medium term, they need to know which onboarding improvement to prioritize which feature have high value but low trial adoption  

3. In the long term, they need a reliable activation metric that they can track over time as a proxy for the health of their trial funnel  

### Trade-offs

There must be a balance against optimizing for conversion and after conversion
